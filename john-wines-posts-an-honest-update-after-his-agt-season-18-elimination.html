John Wines Posts An Honest Update After His 'AGT' Season 18 Elimination

Season 18 of America’s Got Talent has been a rollercoaster of emotions for contestants and viewers alike. Among the remarkable performers who graced the stage was John Wines, an older guitarist whose journey came to a close in the semi-finals. With a mix of gratitude, introspection, and a dash of controversy, he took to Instagram to share his thoughts on his AGT experience.

John Wines Reflects On His AGT Elimination

Wines took AGT by storm with his electrifying performances that showcased not only his exceptional guitar skills but also his infectious passion for music. Hailing from the UK, the 59-year-old guitar tutor and viral TikToker left a lasting impact on the show.

While his AGT journey might have ended in the semi-finals, but his gratitude for the opportunity and the support he received throughout the competition shines through. In a recent Instagram update, Wines shared his reflections as he prepared to head back home after his memorable run on the show.

“Hi guys. So just a quick update. Uh, we’re just waiting to be picked up, ready to go to the airport to go home,” Wines said. “Am I disappointed I’m not going through? Yeah, a little bit. I wouldn’t be human if I wouldn’t.”

Although he was admittedly disappointed with the outcome, the talented guitarist showcased his resilience and appreciation for the journey. While his run on AGT might have ended, Wines’ musical journey continues as he sets his sights on forming a band and exploring new collaborations.

John Wines Addressed the Simon Cowell Controversy

Wines’ journey was not without its share of controversy, largely centered around a critique from judge Simon Cowell. During his performance, Wines wowed the audience and fellow judges, but Cowell’s comments didn’t sit well with fans.

Cowell’s remarks were perceived as rude and condescending, causing a backlash among viewers who disagreed with his assessment. Wines addressed the controversy in his Instagram update.

“Am I bothered about the Simon Cowell thing? Uh, no, not really. Um, just prerogatives this show.” Wines said. “I don’t know why he said what he did. I certainly don’t know why he said about my wife.”

He opened up referring to Cowell’s comments about his wife’s potential reaction to the performance. Wines also empathetically recognized the immense pressure of performing live, and what he could’ve done to make it better.
